Learning Style Test

Read the following story and choose the option that best describes how you would prefer to handle each situation. At the end of the story, your learning style will be revealed based on your selections.

Story: The Adventure of the Lost Artifact

Part 1: The Discovery
You are part of an archaeological team that has discovered an ancient artifact buried in a remote jungle. As you approach the artifact, you need to document your findings.

1. How do you document the artifact?

Take detailed notes and sketches
Record a video describing the artifact and its surroundings
Create a 3D model of the artifact using materials you find around you

Part 2: The Puzzle
You find that the artifact is locked in a chest with a complex puzzle. To open it, you need to solve the puzzle.

2. How do you approach solving the puzzle?

Read the instructions carefully and think through the solution
Discuss possible solutions with your team and brainstorm ideas
Manipulate the puzzle pieces physically, trying different combinations until it opens

Part 3: The Map
Inside the chest, you find a map leading to another part of the jungle where more artifacts are hidden. The map is old and difficult to read.

3. How do you interpret the map?

Study the map thoroughly, noting every detail
Listen to the team leader's interpretation of the map and follow their directions
Use a combination of landmarks and physical exploration to navigate

Part 4: The Obstacle
On your way to the next site, you encounter a river that you need to cross.

4. How do you cross the river?

Look for a bridge or a safe place to wade across
Discuss with your team to come up with the safest crossing strategy
Build a makeshift raft or use a rope to swing across

Part 5: The Artifact Room
You finally reach a hidden room filled with artifacts. You need to catalog all the items quickly.

5. How do you catalog the items?

Write detailed descriptions and take photos
Record a video tour of the room, explaining each artifact
Arrange the artifacts in groups based on their type and take measurements

Part 6: The Excavation
One of the artifacts is buried deep underground and needs to be excavated carefully.

6. How do you handle the excavation?

Study the excavation manual and follow the procedures meticulously
Discuss the best excavation methods with your team
Start digging carefully, feeling your way through the soil and adjusting your technique as needed

Part 7: The Analysis
You need to analyze the materials used in the artifacts to determine their origin.

7. How do you conduct the analysis?

Use a microscope and reference books to study the materials
Listen to an expert’s lecture on similar artifacts and materials
Perform hands-on experiments to test the properties of the materials

Part 8: The Preservation
The artifacts need to be preserved for transport back to your research facility.

8. How do you preserve the artifacts?

Wrap them carefully in protective materials and label each one
Record a detailed video guide on how to handle and preserve the artifacts
Build custom storage boxes to fit each artifact snugly and safely

Part 9: The Presentation
You are tasked with presenting your findings to a group of scholars.

9. How do you prepare your presentation?

Create detailed slides with images and text
Practice your speech and present your findings verbally
Bring replicas or models of the artifacts to demonstrate your findings

Part 10: The Reflection
After the expedition, you reflect on what you learned and how you can improve.

10. How do you reflect on your experience?

Write a detailed report summarizing the expedition
Discuss the experience with your team and get their feedback
Recreate key moments from the expedition to understand what worked and what didn’t
